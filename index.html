Overview

About
the Challenge

This challenge takes you from the first time you open a score to the day you can play through at your target tempo. It is a neuroscience-based system, not a collection of tips.

Key Vocabulary — Read This First

Batches, Passages, and Chunks

These three words come up throughout the challenge. Think of it like a meal:

🍽️
Batch
A full meal — a collection of passages you're working on
🥗
Passage
One dish — a section of your piece (a phrase or measure group)
🥄
Chunk
The bites you take — the tiny units you practice within a passage
✦
This challenge covers
  • ✓Learning a passage from scratch
  • ✓Cleaning up a passage you know halfway
  • ✓Building speed and accuracy in specific sections
  • ✓Identifying exactly what is stopping you and fixing it
Not covered
  • ○Sightreading
  • ○Performance prep (nerves, stagecraft, memorization for the stage)
  • ○Music theory
  • ○Playing for fun without a specific goal

The Framework

The 4 Pillars of Learning New Repertoire Faster

📖
Meaning

See the sections fast and build musicality from day one. You work on music that matters to you.

🎵
Flow

Train leaps and speed early so your music flows without pauses, even in the big jumps. Challenging but doable.

🧠
Retention

Use tiny chunks, mental play, and optimal break schedules so your progress moves into long-term memory.

⏱️
Focus

Stop mindless repeats and steer each session so every minute counts. Short and intentional beats long and unfocused.

A Question People Often Have

Does “Learn New Repertoire Faster” mean I have to work on a brand new piece?

No. Here is what qualifies.
  • ✓A piece you have been working on for months
  • ✓A piece you know halfway but want cleaner and faster
  • ✓A piece you are re-learning after a long break
  • ✓A piece you can play through, but not at your target tempo yet
The challenge will teach you the best ways to practice

Everything from the first time you open the score to the day you can play it at your target tempo. Any stage of learning is fine — not just pieces you have never touched before.

Not covered in this challenge
  • ○Sightreading (playing a piece for the first time with no preparation)
  • ○Performing (managing nerves, memorization for the stage, stagecraft)
  • ○Music theory study
  • ○Playing for fun with no specific goal

If you are still unsure:
Is there anything in this piece that doesn't sound how you want it to? If yes — that is exactly what this challenge is for.

A Note Before You Begin

If you're feeling
behind or unsure.

Read this. It applies to you.

  • It is OK if you didn't practice today. Post your check-in anyway. Showing up counts.
  • It is OK if you only identified the problem without fixing it. Noticing is the first step.
  • It is OK if your tracker shows very little activity. The tracker shows what you need to work on, not how bad you are.
  • It is OK if you are working on a piece you have had for years. That counts.
  • It is OK no matter what level you're at. This system works whether you're intermediate, advanced, or anywhere in between. Rebecca or someone in the group will help you.
  • It is OK if your progress feels slow. Slow, focused practice is what makes things stick. That is the whole point.
